Full Stack Development

Full Stack Development is an approach to web application development that covers both the front-end and back-end. Full Stack developers have the rules and skills to build complex and physical applications by working with different technologies and systems at different levels.

What does full stack development involve?

  • Front-End: Developing the user interface and user experience of applications. This includes languages and frameworks such as HTML, CSS, JavaScript, React, Angular, and Vue.js. The front-end developer’s job is to make the site or application appear and be usable for the user.

  • Back-End: Working with the server, database, and application logic. Development includes technologies such as Node.js, Python, Ruby, PHP, Java, and databases (e.g. MySQL, PostgreSQL, MongoDB). The backend developer is responsible for the logic of the system, data storage and security.

  • DevOps and infrastructure: Deployment and configuration of servers, management of cloud services, automation of processes. Here you can use tools such as Docker, Kubernetes, AWS, CI/CD.

Benefits Full-featured development

Broad horizons: Full Stack — developers understand the entire development cycle, which helps to effectively solve problems and offer comprehensive solutions.

Flexibility: they can switch between the client and server parts, which makes the development process more flexible and dynamic.

Economic resources: one team can solve problems at all levels, which requires the involvement of additional specialists.

Effective time management: Full Stack developers have knowledge in various fields, which allows them to flexibly manage tasks and prioritize work at all levels of development.

Who is Full Stack Development for?

Full Stack Development is suitable for companies and startups that value flexibility and speed of development, as well as developers who want to be universal specialists.
